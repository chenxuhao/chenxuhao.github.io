I am a Research Scientist in the Computation Structures Group at MIT CSAIL, working with Prof. Arvind. I work on parallel system and computer architecture for AI and Big-data, with a focus on data mining and machine learning on graphs. Before joining MIT, I was a research fellow (from Jan. 2019 to Aug. 2020) in the Intelligent Software Systems group at University of Texas at Austin, and a visiting Ph.D. student (from Nov. 2012 to Nov. 2014) in the IMPACT Research group at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.

Research Experience

2020 - Present

Hardware Accelerator for Graph Pattern Mining
With Prof. Arvind
Design and implemented FlexMiner, a pattern-aware accelerator for graph pattern mining (GPM). FlexMiner offers an order of magnitude speedup over state-of-the-art software GPM solutions.

2019 - 2020

Programming Framework for Graph Pattern Mining
With Prof. Keshav Pingali
Design and implemented two graph pattern mining (GPM) frameworks, Pangolin and Sandslash. Pangolin targets both CPU and GPU. It is the first GPM system that supports GPU mining, and it is orders-of-magnitude faster than previous GPM systems. Sandslash targets CPU only. It provides a novel two-level programming interface and supports adavanced GPM optimizations, which offers 8x speedup over Pangolin on CPU.

2015 - 2018

Parallel Graph Algorithms on GPU
With Prof. Zhiying Wang
Design and implemented various parallel graph algorithms, frameworks and benchmarks on the GPU, including vertex coloring, strongly connected components and sparse neural networks.

2011 - 2014

Cache Architecture for Irregular Algorithms on GPU
With Prof. Wen-Mei Hwu and Prof. Zhiying Wang
Designed and implemented efficient cache architectures for irregular applications on GPU.
